Who’s the best basketball player on the planet? Warriors’ Kevin Durant? Lakers’ LeBron James? Raptors’ Kawhi Leonard?

Updated May 10, 2019; Posted May 10, 2019
By Todderick Hunt | NJ Advance Media for NJ.com

An age-old debate rages on as the 2019 NBA playoffs approach the conference finals.

Who’s the best basketball player on the planet?

According to SportsInsider.com, it’s still Los Angeles Lakers forward LeBron James, who missed the playoffs for the first time since 2005. James has had, arguably, his worst NBA season, despite averaging 27.4, 8.5 rebounds and 8.3 assists over 55 games.

He missed much of the season with a groin strain and never fully recovered his mojo, even after returning to the team, and was ultimately shut down before season’s end.

Sixers-Raptors Game 7 predictions

The Philadelphia 76ers had a dominant Game 6 victory over the Toronto Raptors, winning 112-101.

Even when he did play, he didn’t quite dominate games they way he has for for the past decade-plus.

But he’s been atop the league so long, he’s earned good will among fans expecting a return to form in 2019, once the Lakers add pieces around him.

As you can see below, James was voted best in 24 states, 10 more than Kevin Durant, who came in second with 14, coming off two consecutive NBA Finals MVPs with a chance at a third this June.

The map above is based on geo-tagged Twitter data from the last 2 months (over 500,000 tweets) that track discussion of the “best” NBA player.

Houston Rockets guard James Harden came in third, winning 10 states, following one of the top scoring seasons in decades.

Milwaukee Bucks forward Giannis Antetokounmpo came in fourth, winning two states as he continues to climb the NBA’s popularity ladder.

Speaking of popularity, where’s Stephen Curry, one of the most popular players in the NBA?

Well, he didn’t win any states.

I guess they have some work to do.
