Where every Super Bowl MVP went to college
LSU defeats Clemson 42-25 to win CFP National Championship

The Kansas City Chiefs and San Francisco 49ers played in Super Bowl LIV on Sunday, Feb. 2 in Miami, and all 106 roster members active for the Super Bowl were former student-athletes at either the NCAA Division I or II level.

Texas Tech's Patrick Mahomes became the 54th Super Bowl MVP in NFL history, as the Chiefs defeated the 49ers 31-20 for their first Super Bowl victory in 50 years. He is Texas Tech's first Super Bowl MVP winner.

Super Bowl MVPs have come from colleges big and small, from the Division II ranks to powerhouses like Alabama. One has come from FCS school Delaware, and a few played their college football at Georgia.

Chiefs quarterback Mahomes, a former Red Raider, earned the MVP honor after completing 26 of 42 passes for 286 yards, two touchdowns and two interceptions in Kansas City's victory.
