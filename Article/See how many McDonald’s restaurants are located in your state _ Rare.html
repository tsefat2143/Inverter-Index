McDonald’s is easily the¬†world’s largest restaurant chain. The Golden Arches boasts nearly 16,000 locations in the United States alone. But that love is not distributed equally.

24/7 Wall St. did the math to determine how many McDonald’s restaurants are located in each state, and the number of locations per population density.

The very first McDonald’s opened in San Bernardino, Calif., in 1940. (Its building was torn down in 1972, but some of its items are housed in the¬†Original McDonald’s Museum at 1398 N.E. St.) Decades later, California still holds the crown for the largest number of McDonald’s locations in America. However, due to its enormous population, it also has the third smallest number of McDonald’s restaurants per 100,000 residents.

So which state is tops when it comes to number of McDonald’s per 100,000? It’s Ohio, which has 823 Mickey D’s for its 11.5+ million residents.
